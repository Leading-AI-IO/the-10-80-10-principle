## 📖 Overview

According to Gallup's longitudinal study of 22,368 U.S. workers, 46% now use AI at work. Yet only 12% use it daily. **The vast majority tried it once and moved on.**

But the real issue is not whether you can use AI.

**The issue is whether you have designed the division of roles between yourself and AI — and that design determines the quality and quantity of your output.**

Imagine hiring a $200K strategy consultant and asking them to check the weather. McKinsey reports that 65% of organizations regularly use generative AI, yet 44% have experienced negative outcomes. It is not an AI performance problem. The design of how humans and AI collaborate is fundamentally broken.

This book offers a simple answer.

**The first 10% — humans set the direction. The middle 80% — AI executes at scale. The last 10% — humans refine and judge.**

By consciously designing this ratio, individual output reaches 5x the conventional level in both quality and quantity. This is not theory. The author practiced this principle, verified the dramatic improvement in intellectual productivity, and then codified it.

**This book provides the "Thinking OS" for the AI era.**

Tools change. Models evolve. But the structural principle of 10:80:10 — humans own direction and judgment, AI accelerates execution — remains constant as long as the AI era endures. Drawing on the HBS×BCG field experiment with 758 participants, Stanford SCALE's 453-participant writing study, and GitHub's enterprise-scale research, this book dissects every layer of this Thinking OS through the dual lenses of cognitive science, organizational theory, creativity research, and practical Before/After scenarios.
